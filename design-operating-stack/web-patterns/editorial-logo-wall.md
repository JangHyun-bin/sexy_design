# Editorial Logo Wall

## Use When

Use when real institutions, customers, publications, or partners are a major trust
signal.

## Anatomy

- small label
- logo row or grid
- hairline separators
- optional stat field nearby
- optional quote or institutional statement

## Layout Rules

- Logos should be monochrome or intentionally restrained.
- Use hairlines or whitespace instead of cards.
- Do not place logo walls before the page gives users a reason to care unless the
  brands are the message.

## Typography Rules

- Label can be uppercase micro type.
- Surrounding copy should be editorial and specific.
- Avoid loud captions under every logo.

## Surface Rules

- Warm paper, dark technical, or institutional rust fields work well.
- The wall should feel like proof, not decoration.

## Responsive Behavior

- Collapse to two or three columns on mobile.
- Preserve legibility and spacing.
- Do not horizontally scroll logos unless the motion is the concept.

## Anti-Slop Checks

- Fails if logos are fake.
- Fails if logos are generic placeholders.
- Fails if the grid looks like a sponsor block with no context.
